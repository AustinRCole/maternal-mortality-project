Forecast Future MMR in the United States Using Trained Machine Learning Models

Note: Flowchart of the process of creating the MMR 10-year forecast for both race and non-race dataset.

Dataset Stratified by Non-Race and Non-Ethnicity Summary

As the graph presented below, it shows maternal mortality ratio (MMR) from 2009 to 2030. MMR from 2009 to 2019 is an average value across the entire dataset grouped by year. MMR from 2020 to 2030 is calculated by machine learning linear regression and time-series models based on health outcome, insurance coverage and population factors which are calculated by time-series models. For both time-series and linear regression (LR) models predict that U.S.'s MMR will increase in the future. LR predicts MMR in 2030 at 45.2 which is approximately 26% increased from average MMR 2019. Addition, Time-series has predicted the MMR lower than LR by 3% at the year of 2030.

Dataset Stratified by Race and Ethnicity Summary

As the graph presented below, it shows maternal mortality ratio (MMR) from 2009 to 2030 for each race and ethnicity. MMR from 2009 to 2030 is an average value across the entire dataset grouped by year, race and ethnicity. MMR from 2020 to 2030 is calculated by machine learning linear regression models based on population by race and ethnicity which is calculated by time-series model. The line graphs show that MMR for every race and ethnicity will decrease. However, there is only one factor, population by race used to predict MMR. The population tends to increase at a reduced rate. That might be a reason for the decreasing of MMR for each race and ethnicity.

Forecast Summary

The future prediction for each year for both data stratified or not stratified by race and ethnicity shows the straight line because the predictions are calculated by linear regression models. The models have moderate accuracy of prediction (mentioned in the race and non-race model page). Therefore, the outcome of model predictions are unsatisfactory.

If there are additional resources and sufficient time, more data from different sources; for example, household income, number of maternal healthcare professionals, will be collected in order to find any correlation to maternal deaths. Additionally, if more data is collected the possibility of finding the most significant factor will be higher. Eventually, there will be a policy in order to decrease the maternal mortality event from happening in the future.
